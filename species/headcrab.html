Headcrab


"Have you ever seen such a magnificent species? These crabs can completely control their hosts' nervous system. Can you imagine what the next stage of mutation looks like?" ― Black Mesa scientist
Headcrabs are studied at Black Mesa, notably in the Advanced Biological Research Lab and the Gamma Labs.

Headcrabs are fairly small creatures, roughly the size of a pumpkin, which crawl on four legs, and have enough jumping power to leap up to 10 feet (3 meters) in any direction. Their underbelly is almost completely covered by a large, lipless mouth with a hidden "beak" used for feeding and latching onto the skulls of victims.
Headcrabs are a parasitic species, attaching to the host's head and clamping onto their skull using their sharp beak. After breaking through the skull, the Headcrab proceeds to take over its host's motor functions through some unknown biological process, keeping its host alive but helpless through the entire process. It seems to take from several seconds to minutes for a Headcrab to turn humans into Zombies. As no humans live in Xen, the headcrab's homeworld, it would seem unlikely that they would evolve such abilities if there were no hosts. Therefore, it is very likely that they can host Xen creatures as well, such as Vortigaunts. The host appears to retain partial consciousness once the Headcrab has taken control, Zombines retain a certain ability to talk. Each Headcrab variation affects its host in its own way, producing a Zombie extremely distinct from the other types in both appearance and behavior.
